Our Story

Organisation Overview

Visionary Minds Community Based Organisation was founded in 2019 by a group of passionate young Kenyans who recognised that marginalised communities — particularly youth and women — were being left behind in economic and social development.

We are registered under the Ministry of Public Service, Gender, and Affirmative Action as a Community Based Organisation, giving us the mandate to operate across Nairobi County and beyond.

Today, Visionary Minds implements six flagship programs, has mobilised over 20 active members, and continues to expand its reach — driven by a vision of financially independent, socially responsible, and empowered communities.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

Empowering Communities Through Action

To empower youth and women through knowledge-sharing, financial inclusion, and collective action in order to improve livelihoods and promote sustainable community development.

Our Vision

A Future of Empowered Individuals

To be a leading community-based organisation fostering empowered, financially independent, and socially responsible individuals who actively contribute to the development of their communities and the nation of Kenya.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

Seven principles that guide every decision, program, and interaction at Visionary Minds.

Transparency

We operate openly, sharing information with members, partners, and the public to build trust.

Integrity

We uphold the highest ethical standards and act honestly in all our endeavours.

Accountability

We take full responsibility for our actions, outcomes, and the resources entrusted to us.

Inclusiveness

We welcome everyone regardless of gender, tribe, religion, or economic background.

Commitment

We are unwavering in our dedication to our mission and the communities we serve.

Confidentiality

We protect the privacy and sensitive information of our members and stakeholders.

Respect

We treat every person with dignity, honour their voice, and value diverse perspectives.

Strategic Focus

Our Objectives

Twelve concrete goals — as set out in Article 3B of our constitution — that translate our mission into everyday action and measurable outcomes.

1

To empower youth and women through financial literacy, entrepreneurship training, and leadership development.

2

To promote a culture of saving and investment through table banking, merry-go-round, and collective investment initiatives.

3

To mobilize resources for sustainable community development projects, including agribusiness, small enterprises, and innovation.

4

To provide members with access to affordable credit facilities to support business growth and personal development.

5

To build members' capacity in data-driven decision-making, research, and strategic planning.

6

To create a support network that fosters mentorship, accountability, and personal growth among members.

7

To initiate and support income-generating activities that benefit both members and the wider community.

8

To promote integrity, transparency, and accountability in financial and organizational management.

9

To partner with like-minded organizations, institutions, and stakeholders to advance community development goals.

10

To promote social responsibility and community service, including education, environmental conservation, and civic awareness.

11

To enhance digital skills and access to opportunities in online work, innovation, and technology.

12

To support vulnerable groups within the community through welfare initiatives and empowerment programs.

Looking Ahead

Our Future Vision

By 2030, Visionary Minds aspires to be a nationally recognised community development organisation, scaling its programs to reach every county in Kenya.

We envision a network of financially independent savings groups, a digital platform connecting mentors with mentees, and a youth innovation hub that incubates community-led enterprises.

Our long-term goal is to influence policy, partner with county governments, and create a replicable model of community empowerment that can be adopted across East Africa.
